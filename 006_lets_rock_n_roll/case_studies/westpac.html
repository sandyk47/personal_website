The Westpac Business Sentiment Survey (WBSS) is a comprehensive report that uncovers business sentiments, trends, and demographics concerning Australian businesses operating in China. The report is a collaboration between the Australian Chamber of Commerce Shanghai (AustCham Shanghai), Westpac Banking Corporation, and China Skinny. It encapsulated data from 211 valid responses, analyzed across 65 intricate questions. It provides valuable insights into the fabric of Australian business dealings and business sentiment with respect to China - Australia‚Äôs largest trading partner.

The WBSS seeks to gain deeper insights into the business sentiment and strategies of Australian businesses operating within China. The complexity of China‚Äôs market environment, combined with the diverse range of Australian businesses operating therein, poses a challenge. Accurate, actionable insights are crucial to help Australian enterprises navigate this market effectively and to equip them with strategies to succeed.

2. Project Management

To ensure the success of the WBSS project, a systematic and structured approach to project management was crucial. I turned to the fundamentals laid out by Chris Croft's Project Management Fundamentals course as a foundation, tailoring it to the unique requirements and challenges posed by this specific initiative.

Steps Undertaken:

  1. Success Criteria & Constraints Agreement:
    Collaborated closely with clients to mutually agree upon the success criteria and major constraints. Ensured these agreements were documented in writing, providing a clear roadmap and benchmark for project success.
  2. Task Listing:
    Enumerated all tasks required for the successful completion of the project. This helped in visualizing the project's roadmap and key milestones.
  3. Time & Cost Estimation:
    Allocated realistic timeframes and budget estimates to each task, setting the stage for efficient resource utilization.
  4. Dependency Assessment & Critical Paths:
    Recognized dependencies between tasks and identified critical paths. This assessment enabled the projection of the overall project timeline, helping in setting realistic delivery expectations.
  5. Gantt Chart Construction:
    Built a Gantt chart to visualize the project timeline, task dependencies, and key milestones. This served as the central tool for tracking project progress.
  6. Time & Resource Calculations:
    Based on task estimates, deduced the time and resource requirements, ensuring the timely availability of necessary assets and manpower.
  7. Progress Monitoring:
    Regularly checked project progression against the Gantt chart. This kept the project on track and aided in identifying any potential delays or challenges.
  8. Stakeholder Liaison:
    Kept in constant contact with key stakeholders from all teams, ensuring a harmonized approach. Regular reviews of the report were undertaken to guarantee alignment and to incorporate feedback.
  9. Report Delivery:
    Delivered report in its hard copy format, and presented to key stakeholders. This involved public speaking and launch events, ensuring the research's findings reached a wide audience and achieved maximum impact.
  10. Learning Review:
    Post-project, conducted a thorough review of learnings. This introspection paved the way for improvements in future projects.

3. Research & Analysis

The research phase was crucial to underpinning the entire WBSS project, ensuring that all findings and recommendations were rooted in factual, up-to-date insights about the intricate China market and the Australian businesses operating within it.

Desk & Field Research üî¨

Comprehensively reviewed existing literature, reports, and other secondary data sources. Additionally, conducted on-ground field research to gather firsthand insights, enabling a richer, more nuanced understanding of the landscape.

Case Study Interviews üí¨

Orchestrated and conducted in-depth case study interviews with relevant business executives in the space. These interviews offered granular insights, capturing the nuances of different businesses' experiences in the China market.

Quantitative Analysis üìä

The foundation of our research was a meticulously crafted survey that sought to encompass the breadth and depth of experiences and perceptions related to the China market.

  • Survey Execution: Executed rollout of a structured survey, ensuring questions were consistent, balanced, clear, and objective. The survey, hosted by Wenjuanxing, was opened for a span of six weeks, and we successfully garnered 211 valid responses across 65 diverse questions.
  • Response Analysis: Each response was subjected to a thorough statistical analysis. This involved segregating data into discernible patterns, cross-referencing responses for consistency, and identifying significant trends or anomalies.
  • Interpretation: Beyond the raw numbers, we dove deep into what these statistics meant in the context of the broader market environment. Factors such as market fragmentation, different business structures in China, revenue ranges, and industry breakdowns were carefully interpreted to derive meaningful insights.
  • Integration: After interpretation, findings were seamlessly integrated into the broader report. The quantitative data served as the backbone, providing hard evidence that complemented and supported insights from other research methods.

6. Stakeholder Management

Stakeholder management was a crucial aspect of the WBSS project. Given the diverse and high-profile nature of the involved entities, establishing clear communication, managing expectations, and ensuring alignment across all parties became imperative.

Approach to Stakeholder Management

  • Identification & Prioritisation: Started by recognising the primary stakeholders across Westpac, AustCham Shanghai, China Skinny and various other entities mentioned in the report. By understanding the unique needs and priorities of each group, time, resources and attention were appropriately allocated.
  • Clear Communication Channels: Set up regular touchpoints and updates with all key stakeholders via WeChat, email and fortnightly catch-up meetings. This was done to ensure that everyone was on the same page and to address concerns or questions in real-time.
  • Feedback Integration: To produce a report that resonated with all audiences, feedback from Westpac and AustCham Shanghai was actively sought and integrated. This iterative approach ensured that the final document was a reflection of collective input and met the primary objectives set out at the project's inception.
  • Presentations & Launch Events: Beyond the written report, there were also public speaking and launch events. Here, the importance was not just on delivering the content but also on ensuring that it was presented in a way that aligned with stakeholder expectations and resonated with the audience.
  • Post-Project Review: After the report's delivery, a review meeting was conducted with all stakeholders to gather learnings, discuss the project's success areas, and understand potential areas for improvement in future engagements.
